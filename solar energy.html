KEY TO RETRIEVE THE COLLAPSING ECONOMY THROUGH RENEWABLE ENERGY

The pandemic situation of Covid-19 has compelled the world to think more about sustainable energy due to adverse declination of the world's economy and environmental conditions. According to reports, a country like India spends 1.5 trillion dollars annually to import coal for production of energy,which can be saved if we will,think and plan a road map to use solar energy as a primary source of electricity in our country.

As India lies in a temperate and torrid zone where we have 300 clear sunny days, we have shown real commitment to use clean green renewable energy for economic growth. India has achieved 5th global position in solar power development with an 11 times increase in solar power capacity from 2.6 GW to 30 GW in the last five years and aims to take this to 100 GW production by 2022. Currently our government is providing 40% subsidy for less than 3kW and 20% for 4kW - 10kW on rooftop and farm installation of solar panels. But we are facing major challenges in manufacturing solar equipment, mainly semiconductors and batteries. Silicon plays an important role while manufacturing the solar panels which is majorly imported from China because no Indian company is involved in silicon production which increases the cost of solar wafers.Hence, India should majorly focus on production of solar panels in the upcoming years.

PM Modi’s mission of ‘One World One Sun One Grid’, where we are planning to connect about 140 countries, majorly which lies in equator and torrid zone together for production of electricity through solar energy globally by taking financial help from world bank and Asian development bank.
If the mission gets successful we will achieve self reliance in following ways-

  • Employment generation due to increase in manufacturing of solar equipment
  • Rural development due to solar farming
  • Reduction in fuel import bills and dependency on oil producing countries
  • Clean Environment and enhanced quality of life.

With investment of 1.4 billion dollars and total capacity of 2245 MW, Bhadla Solar Park is the largest solar park in the world as of 2020, and is spread over a total area of 5,700 hectares in Jodhpur,Rajasthan.

Also Cochin International airport has created history by becoming first airport in the world that completely operates on solar power, comprising of 46,150 solar panels laid across 45 acres and rooftops of car parking, which produces 50,000 to 60,000 units of electricity per day to be consumed for all its operational functions by saving 30 to 40 thousand rupees daily.

Initially focusing on large/commercial scale like airports, railway stations, barren lands,etc then for household purposes, we can save millions of rupees in coming years.
