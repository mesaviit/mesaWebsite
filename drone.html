DRONE TECHNOLOGY

The technology of drones has been developed gradually in past years, they are used in various sectors like agriculture , industrial, construction, Military, delivery, emergency rescue ,etc . And have been a handful in terms of usage. Drones have been used since 1849 and used in world war for aerial advantage in the battle , in olden times it is specifically used by the military but in modern times it's used for aerial photography , for film companies, construction sites , delivery etc. Let's see how it's going to be used in future and how it's gonna make our lives easy

What are drones ?
So , let's understand the concept of drones. Drones are basically an unmanned aerial vehicle or aircraft without a pilot. It includes additionally a ground based controller and a system of communication.Drones can carry out different and various range of tasks, it can perform tasks from military operations to package delivery and can be of any size . Its size ranges from as large as aircraft to as small as a palm of hand. In the land, sea, air, space where the man is unable to see the places and cannot go these drones are very useful in tracking things as it can go anywhere and take a photo or record video it has become an eyes and ears of the user as it is controlled by the person in the ground or by a computer program.

How do drones work ?
The unmanned aerial vehicles( UAV) or commonly known as drones work on the propulsion system which includes motors, electronic speed controller and propellers . Which makes the UAV fly in air and can control or move in any direction.Light composite material is used to manufacture drones thus the weight is reduced and it can move freely. The drones or UAV are equipped with various components like GPS, infrared camera and laser beams which are used in military operations. It generally has a two parts control system and the drone itself . All the navigational , sensors and control systems are present in the nose of the drone and the body has a drone technology system that has no space for human accommodation. Basically the propeller receives data from the and then sends to the electronic speed controller On the drone motor to either fly or stop . The electronic speed controller gives the following signal to the drone motor to the information on speed ,braking and monitor the fault tolerance on the drone motor .

Future aspects

The drone technology has been there since old times and is still in use for various purposes; it has been a commonplace for business and non profit sectors . In the near future the use of unmanned aircraft will be widely spread.
The different sectors where this technology can revolutionize our near future are :
Agriculture: some agencies already use drones to manage livestock and in the future farmers can use drones to monitor and spray chemicals and water to their crops .
Conservation: the unmanned aerial vehicle can be used to monitor endangered species and thus get track of ecosystems. Thus it will expand as technology advances.
Delivery: Anything that a postman delivers drone can deliver in the same manner and in a faster way, in the near future it can be Used on a daily basis.

Disaster management and relief: Drones can go places where humans can't enter in this situation drones are a big help in monitoring and rescuing people . And delivering emergency supplies to remote places is disaster areas.
Logistics: drones that can deliver heavy loads can replace trucks in inventory management and can move goods in warehouses . This can reduce the number of semi in roads.
Filmmaking and photography: At some level filmmakers are already using drones to film and take photographs to capture shots from aerial view. It's been use widely in live tv and sports .
Law enforcement: police can use this to monitor criminal practices in regional area where it is difficult to track them.

As the technology advances in the future it will be used in many industries and be profitable in business. The drone industry is growing rapidly over the years and in a few years we can see drones in our near sky .
