IPL : A MONEY SPINNER

UNDERSTANDING IPL BUSINESS MODEL

With a perfect mixture of cricket, bollywood and big money Indian Primer League (IPL) is the most-attended cricket league in the world. With a colossal brand value of 475 billion rupees, IPL has one of the most fascinating and structured business models of the century. Inspired from European football leagues but customized for India by Lalit Modi and BCCI, IPL became popular right from its first season which started in 2008 consisting of 8 teams, to get a connect of soil to the audience they are named after 8 cities of India.

But how does IPL team owners (franchise) and BCCI makes money through it?
A good business has many sources of income, so there are various sources from which they make money lets list out them-

  1. Broadcasting Right-

    2. 264 million viewers tunned in to watch IPL on TVs in 2019, the biggest chunk is fulfilled by broadcasting channels by taking media and telecasting rights by paying some amount to BCCI, further some of this amout is distributed to teams. There are biddings of various channels and the highest bidder wons the rights. Initially the rights were purchased by SONY Entertainment for 8200 crores for period of 10 years.Currently the media rights are purchased by STAR INDIA for 16347 crores (3269 per year) for tenure of 5 years (2018-2022). IPL generates its 60-70% revenue from broadcasting rights.

  2. Sponsorships-
    • Title sponsorship-

      • Due to its increasing audience, many brands want their name shoud be present in the title itsef for advertisement purposes. DLF and Pepsi were the title sponsers from 2008 to 2017, Vivo won the bid for title sponsorship and paid 2199 croress for tenure of 5 years which is 439.8/year. Currently Dream11 has title sponsorship for 2020 by paying 222 crores.

    • Brand sponsorship-

      • Many multi national, national and local companies advertise their orginazitations logo on jerseys of the players, statium boards, pitch, boundry lines,etc. The logos printed on the chest area are the most expensive places for the sponsors. This is the major source of income for teams and stadium as well.

  3. Ticket sales-

    4. On an average of 4 crores is earned every match through selling tickets, from which major chunk goes to home team (owner) and some amount is paid to the cricket association of that particular stadium.

  4. Merchandise sales-

    5. Many teams sells their merchandise like caps,wrist watches,T-shirts,etc. All this money goes to the team owners. Player trading is also a good source of income to the owners,many new and young players gets chance to showcase their talent to the world.

  5. Prize Money-

    6. Probably this is the smallest chunk of money which is earned by team. In 2019 this was 20 crores for winner and 12.5 croress for runner up team. Half of the money will go to the franchise whereas the other half will be distributed among the players.

Franchise Owner’s Investment

For the team owners there are many expenses like player’s bidding, hotel and traveling expenses for the team, their other support staff and coach salaries, administration team,etc.Thse expenses can very from 130-160 crores. Initially it was tough to make profits to the franchises but now a days the media rights increased by 25% and sponsorship revenue has increased by 4 times. Currently Mumbai Indians has the highest total brand value of 809 crores in the IPL followed by Chennai Super Kings of 732 crores. BCCI has very good profits in IPL because of very less investments and good returns, BCCI treasurer has revealed IPL revenue of Rs 4000 crore for year 2020, according to BCCI, the 2015 IPL season contributed ₹11.5 billion (US$160 million) to the GDP of the Indian economy.
